# About Me

I am a PhD student at [RAND](https://rand.edu) and a Non-Resident Fellow at [IFP](https://ifp.org) based in Washington, DC. I focus on artificial intelligence and American national security.

At RAND, I work with [CAST](https://www.rand.org/global-and-emerging-risks/centers/ai-security-and-technology.html) to advise government agencies on frontier AI deployment, developing policy options and technical measures to enhance reliability and security. For my doctoral research, I am comparatively evaluating national defense strategies to counter adversary use of advanced AI.

At IFP, I work on [The Launch Sequence](https://ifp.org/launch), a collection of ambitious projects to prepare for advanced AI. We are actively searching for the most promising ideas and talent to accelerate science, strengthen security, and adapt institutions. If you have any leads, please reach out.

Previously, I graduated from [Georgia Tech](https://www.gatech.edu) with a BS and an MS in Computer Science. I interned in software roles at the Georgia Tech Research Institute, Federal Reserve Board, and Washington Post.
